Camera Model & Geometry

3D → 2D Projection

λ[u v 1]ᵀ = K · [R|t] · [X Y Z 1]ᵀ

K is the 3×3 intrinsic matrix. [R|t] (Pmat) transforms world coordinates to camera space. Radial distortion correction: Δ = (p − o)(k₁r² + k₂r⁴) where r is distance from principal point.

2D → 3D Ray Back-Projection

d = R⁻¹ · K⁻¹ · [u v 1]ᵀ d̂ = d / ‖d‖ (unit ray)

Inverts the intrinsic and rotation matrices to compute the unit direction ray through a pixel. Used as input to two-view triangulation.

Two-View Triangulation

n₁ = d₂ × (d₁ × d₂) n₂ = d₁ × (d₂ × d₁) s₁ = p₁ + [(p₂−p₁)·n₁ / (d₁·n₁)] d₁ X = (s₁ + s₂) / 2

Finds the nearest midpoint between two rays from camera positions p₁ and p₂. The result is the estimated 3D joint position.

Epipolar Lines

e₁ = Project(pos₂, cam₁) ← epipole l₁ = e₁ − joint₂D₁

The epipole is the projection of the opposite camera center into the current image. The epipolar line through each joint points toward the epipole — used for qualitative verification.

Each frame of mocap data contains the 3D positions of all 12 joints. The pipeline projects them to two calibrated camera views (vue2, vue4), reconstructs 3D positions via triangulation, and compares against the ground truth.

Implementation
Project.m 3D → 2D

Transforms world points to camera space via [Pmat; 0001], applies intrinsics [Kmat | 0], performs homogeneous divide, then optionally corrects radial distortion using k₁r² + k₂r⁴ centred on the principal point.

BackProject.m 2D → Ray

Computes R⁻¹K⁻¹[u v 1]ᵀ to get the 3D direction of a pixel, then normalises with vecnorm to produce a unit ray. Used as input to triangulation.

Triangulate.m Triangulation

Given two rays and two camera positions, finds the nearest midpoint via cross-product normals: n₁ = d₂ × (d₁ × d₂), solves for the closest points on each ray, returns (s₁ + s₂) / 2.

ProcessFrame.m Per Frame

Per-frame pipeline: projects all 12 joints to both views, back-projects to rays, triangulates, and computes epipolar lines by projecting each camera's center into the opposite view.

ProcessData.m Batch

Loops over all mocap frames, stores projected points, rays, triangulations, and epipolar lines in pre-allocated matrices. Computes per-frame, per-joint L2 error: ‖triangulated − mocap‖.

QuantitativeAnalysis.m Stats

Filters to frames where all 12 joints are valid. Reports mean, std, median, min, max of L2 error per joint and globally. Plots total error (sum over joints) per frame.

QualitativeAnalysis.m Visual

Reads a specific video frame from each camera, overlays mocap projections in green and re-projected triangulations in red, and draws epipolar lines through each joint — showing alignment between the two views.

DrawSkeleton.m Rendering

Draws right arm (1–3), left arm (4–6), right leg (7–9), left leg (10–12), shoulder bar, hip bar, and spine by plotting 2D joint coordinates connected as limb chains.
